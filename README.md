## About

ICARUS is a browser-based critical game about labor, automation, and the hidden rules of performance under surveillance capitalism.

You are a new employee at **Prometheus Inc.** — a global logistics and technology conglomerate. Your job is simple: do your work, meet your targets, keep your position.

The game never tells you everything you need to know.

---

## How to Play

Open `index.html` in a browser. No installation required.

Use your mouse to interact. Click and drag packages to their correct delivery slots before time runs out. Watch your performance metrics. Read the notifications from HR.

Try to last as long as possible.

---

## The Company

**Prometheus Inc.** operates two divisions:

- **Hermes Logistics** — fulfillment and package delivery
- **Sphinx Systems** — software engineering and systems design *(coming soon)*

Each division has its own logic, its own demands, and its own way of measuring your value.

## Context

ICARUS draws on the mythology of Daedalus and Icarus — the craftsman who built the labyrinth, the wings, and the trap — but reframes the myth within the logic of contemporary labor: platform economies, algorithmic management, automated performance review, and the gradual displacement of human work by machine learning systems.

The game is structured as a piece of tactical media. Its mechanics are its argument. What the player does inside the game is inseparable from what the game is saying about the world outside it.

The title refers not only to the fall, but to the flight itself — to the brief, precarious window between the water and the sun.

## Technical

Built with Phaser.js 3. Runs entirely in the browser — no backend, no data collection, no accounts.

Pixel art assets generated with Google Gemini / Nano Banana and edited for game use.
Audio generated procedurally via Web Audio API.
